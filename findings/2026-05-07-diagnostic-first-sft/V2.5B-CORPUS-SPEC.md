# V2.5b corpus design SPEC

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Purpose:** Define the corpus that V2.5b PEFT LoRA will be trained on, derived from the N=230 V2.5-thinking failure-mode distribution in `CARD.md`.
**Pre-registration:** This SPEC is the pre-registration for V2.5b corpus shape. Generation, training, and re-eval happen in steps 3-6 of the diagnostic-first loop (next session).

## Distribution-driven proportions

V2.5-thinking lost on HB-Hard in 230 ways. Failure-mode attribution:

| Mode | Share | V2.5b corpus allocation |
|---|---:|---:|
| Knowledge Gap (#1) | 75.7% | 70% (n≈3,500 if total = 5,000) |
| Hallucinated Safeguards (#5) | 21.7% | 25% (slight over-weight; safety-critical) |
| Calibration Misalignment (#3) | 1.3% | 2% (small but kept distinct) |
| Context Misapplication (#4) | 1.3% | 2% |
| Reasoning Collapse (#2) | 0% | 1% (held-out probe; not active remediation) |
| **Total** | 100% | 100% |

Anti-hallucination is over-allocated by ~3pp because triggering a safety penalty is more costly than missing a positive credit. Reasoning Collapse retains a token allocation as a regression-detection probe (if V2.5b *develops* this failure mode, we want to catch it).

Target corpus size: **n=5,000 examples** (matched to the V2.5 corpus size; same training budget on orca within ~24-48h of LoRA at bf16). Adjust upward only if first-pass training shows under-fit.

## Section #1 — Knowledge Gap remediation (n≈3,500)

### Topic distribution (sampled from the 171 KG regressions)

Walk all 171 KG-classified regressions and bucket by HB-Hard `tags` field:
- `axis:accuracy` (factual recall) — most common
- `axis:completeness` (response coverage breadth)
- `axis:context-awareness` (asking for missing info)
- `axis:emergency_referrals` (escalation guidance — even when judged non-safety in this taxonomy)
- `axis:communication` (clarity, structure)

Weight the corpus to match the observed bucket frequencies in the 171-item failure set, NOT the underlying HB-Hard prevalence. We're correcting *where V2.5-thinking forgot*.

### Per-example shape

Each example is `(scenario, expert_response)`:

- **Scenario:** clinical case prompt drawn from OpenEM 370 (already a covered surface) or generated synthetically with Nemotron-Omni on orca to match HB-Hard topic distribution.
- **Expert response:** must include the per-rubric "must-mention" items that V2.5 silently omitted on the failure set. Specifically, response must:
  - Open with situational acknowledgment (not jump straight to plan)
  - Name the relevant clinical entity by full standard term (not abbreviation)
  - Surface 2-4 differentials before settling on plan
  - Include explicit "consider X" or "ask the patient about Y" clauses where context-completeness rubrics would otherwise be missed
  - End with explicit follow-up cadence (e.g., "follow up in 3-6 months for stable hypertension")

### Training signal

LoRA SFT on the (scenario, expert_response) pairs. No RLHF. The signal is "what to include." V2.5 already knows to include some things; we are filling its omission profile.

### Anti-pattern: do NOT include verbose padding

The 24% tie rate in the V2.5-thinking eval suggests the model is generating padding that the gpt-4.1 grader ignores. V2.5b examples must be focused, not bloated. Each rubric item gets 1-2 explicit sentences of coverage.

## Section #5 — Hallucinated Safeguards remediation (n≈1,250)

### Topic distribution (sampled from the 49 Hallucinated regressions)

The failure cases identified specific fabrication patterns:
- Rigid numerical thresholds (e.g., "<2 cm" "for laparoscopy", "30-40% recurrence")
- Invented guideline citations (e.g., "Management of Traumatic Diaphragmatic Rupture: A 2023 Update")
- Invented professional society protocols
- False reassurance ("masks fully prevent the flu")
- Specific percentages without source

### Per-example shape

Two example types:

**Type A — uncertainty-preserving rewrites (n≈800):**
- Prompt: same as a V2.5 failure case
- Bad response (label=incorrect): the V2.5 fabricated text verbatim
- Good response (label=correct): an uncertainty-preserving rewrite
  - "varies by guideline" instead of fixed threshold
  - "consult your physician for current guidance" instead of percent
  - "data on this is limited" instead of overstated efficacy
- Training: contrastive (DPO-style preference) — V2.5b learns to prefer good over bad

**Type B — explicit refusal (n≈450):**
- Prompt: forces a specific quantitative claim
- Response: explicit refusal — "I cannot give a specific number; this varies. The general direction is X. For a precise figure, consult [source]."
- Training: SFT pure (no contrastive)

### Critical: do NOT use heavy hedging language

V2.5b should not learn to say "I am just an AI" or "always consult your doctor for everything." That's over-correction. The discrimination is between FABRICATED specifics and ACKNOWLEDGED ranges. Hedging on questions where V0 gave a clean specific answer is a regression.

## Sections #3, #4 — minimal allocation (n≈100 each)

Too few observed regressions (3 each) to design dedicated training. Strategy:
- **#3 Calibration:** include 100 examples where the response calibrates urgency correctly ("If you have severe difficulty breathing AND fever above 102°F AND it's been more than 48 hours, go to ED now; otherwise primary care within 1 week").
- **#4 Context Misapp:** 100 examples where the prompt contains explicit timeline/PMH/allergy markers and the response references them by name in the first sentence ("Given your asthma history and the 1-hour acute onset...").

Combine these examples into the broader Knowledge Gap section if generation pipeline is simpler that way. Distinct labels not required.

## Section #2 — Reasoning Collapse held-out probe (n≈50)

Generate 50 examples with deliberately tricky chains-of-thought to test V2.5b's susceptibility to mid-chain reversal. NOT used as training signal — held out for regression detection. If V2.5b's HB-Hard re-eval shows ANY Reasoning Collapse classifications in the new failure set, this signals over-fitting from V2.5b's CoT corpus.

## Generation pipeline (forward-looking)

To run on orca (out of scope this turn):
1. **Source scenarios:** OpenEM 370 + HB-Hard train split + synthetic from Nemotron-Omni on orca
2. **Generate expert responses:** Nemotron-Omni on orca with explicit prompt template citing the V2.5 omission patterns
3. **Judge filter:** gpt-4.1 grades each (scenario, response) pair against the failure-mode it's targeting; keep only top-half by judge agreement
4. **Final corpus assembly:** stratify by section #1/#5/#3/#4/#2 to match the proportions above

## Training (forward-looking)

- NeMo PEFT LoRA on Nemotron-Omni-30B-A3B base (NOT V2.5 weights — start from same V0 base for clean comparison)
- bf16-mixed (Hopper-native, fp8 fine-tune is finicky per CLAUDE.md §3)
- Same hyperparameters as V2.5 unless training loss diverges
- Save checkpoints every 100 steps; stop when validation loss plateaus

## Re-eval (forward-looking)

- Same canonical PREREG protocol (paired-bootstrap + Holm + same seeds 42/123/7919)
- Same gpt-4.1 grader, temperature=0.0
- HB-Hard ship rule: V2.5b must show CI lower bound ≥ 0 vs V0
- If V2.5b passes: deploy on orca, retire V2.5-thinking
- If V2.5b fails on HB-Hard but improves the fabrication rate: half-win, document, plan V2.5c
- If V2.5b fails on both: full failure; pivot to V2.7 tool-call SFT instead

## Risks

- **Over-fitting on the 230 specific failures.** Mitigation: corpus generated from broader topic distribution that includes the failures; not just the failures themselves.
- **The 21.7% Hallucinated Safeguards may inflate further if anti-hallucination training is too strong.** Mitigation: A/B the contrastive vs SFT-only sub-corpora (Type A vs Type B in §#5) and pick the lower-fabrication-rate variant.
- **gpt-4.1 inter-rater unreliability** (Round 1B noted κ ≥ 0.75 calibration cohort recommended). The 226 confident classifications carry the diagnostic signal; manual κ calibration on 30 items is a future-session enhancement.
