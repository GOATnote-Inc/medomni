## What B300 actually unlocks vs H200

| Spec | H200 | B300 | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Architecture | Hopper SM 9.0 | Blackwell Ultra SM 10.3 | B300 has 5th-gen tensor cores → **NVFP4 native compute at 14 PFLOPS dense** (Hopper stores NVFP4 weights but math runs on FP8/BF16) |
| HBM | 141 GB HBM3 | **288 GB HBM3E** | Co-tenant entire MedOmni stack on one pod |
| HBM bandwidth | 4.8 TB/s | **8 TB/s** | ~1.67× decode-throughput on memory-bound generation |
| Power | 700 W | 1100 W | Heat density doubled |
| Cost (Brev) | $4.24/hr | $8.88/hr | ~2.1× cost for ~5–10× capability on NVFP4 workloads |

For Omni NVFP4 (~21 GB weights) the 288 GB B300 is **dramatic over-provisioning** for inference alone — but exactly right for **co-tenant the whole MedOmni stack on one pod**:

```
Omni-NVFP4              ~21 GB
NV-Embed-v2 fp16        ~14 GB  (or ~7 GB at fp8)
Llama-Nemotron-Rerank-VL ~3.5 GB
Llama-Guard-3-8B fp8    ~8 GB
nx-cugraph KG (in-VRAM)  ~2 GB at 2K nodes; up to ~50 GB at 100K nodes
KV cache (256K context)   ~50–80 GB depending on batch
─────────────────────
total budget              ~150–180 GB used; ~100 GB headroom
```

Single pod, single inference brain, no inter-pod tunneling for retrieval. **The orchestration simplification is itself a capability.**

## Blackwell foot-guns (baked into the B300 deploy script)

`scripts/serve_omni_b300.sh` ships with these workarounds — DO NOT remove them:

1. `--no-async-scheduling` — fixes vLLM V1 engine `cudaErrorIllegalInstruction` at batch>1 on Blackwell+NVFP4 (per [NVIDIA-NeMo/Nemotron#125](https://github.com/NVIDIA-NeMo/Nemotron/issues/125)).
2. `--disable-attention-backend trtllm` — avoids the `is_strictly_contiguous` bug on Blackwell ([vllm-project/vllm#32353](https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm/issues/32353)).
3. `--max-model-len 131072` (default; cap at 262144) — 1M context OOMs.
4. `enable_thinking=false, temperature=0.2` for any audio request — audio + reasoning are mutually exclusive.

CUDA 13 is required for Blackwell. The Brev image should have it; verify in the cold-start log.

## What changes vs v2

- v2 had Brev H200 as canonical inference + retrieval. **v2.5 moves canonical inference to B300**, single-pod, all four retrieval primitives co-tenanted.
- v2 had RunPod H100 as orchestrator + Llama-Guard-3-8B input/output rail. **v2.5 moves the safety rail onto the B300 alongside the inference brain.** The orchestrator stays on RunPod (cheaper there; doesn't need GPU compute — needs only ssh + monitoring tools).
- v2 had Brev H100 as voice gateway. **Unchanged.**
- v2 had Brev H200 in standby for R2 sovereign judge / R3 PEFT LoRA. **v2.5 keeps it for R3 training** when that lands; demoted from active inference role.

## What does NOT change vs v2

- Four-persona architecture (physician / nurse / family / patient).
- Persona-tagged graph schema (six node types, persona_mask edges).
- Retrieval pipeline (10 stages: Llama-Guard-3 → cuVS → rerank → nx-cugraph 2-hop → subgraph slice → Omni → constrained decoding → grounding → FKGL → Llama-Guard-3 output rail).
- Tamoxifen acceptance test (`corpus/clinical-demo/CLN-DEMO-TAMOXIFEN-MIRENA/`).
- The 6 foot-guns + their mitigations (citation-of-authority, persona drift, cross-card synthesis, Mamba2 state saturation, persona prompt-injection, confidently-incorrect on rare conditions).
